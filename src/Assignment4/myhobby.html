About Climbing

Rock climbing is a rapidly growing activity which involves two main types of climbing. First, bouldering is the climbing of smaller "boulders", which features individuals climbing a large rock without any rope. Typically, some sort of crash pad, or large and soft matt, will be place below the face of the rock which is being climbed. This allows the climber to fall onto a soft surface in the event that they fall of the rock. Second, is rope climbing, which has two different methods to safety. First is top rope climbing, where an anchor is set up at the top of the route and a rope fed down to the climber. The climber then has a belayer who will feed the slack out of the rope as they climb, catching them if they should fall. Second is lead climbing, which involves the climber bringing the rope up with them. This type of climbing is seen typically in situations where setting up an anchor is not possible, and requires a much more in depth level of experience and training to properly conduct.

Climbing is a very fun activity, which features more than just physical activity. Climbers must not only have the strength to climb up a route, but have the skill and knowledge to perform certain techniques while climbing. In addition to the ability to complete routes, they must also solve the climb, and solve the puzzle of how to conduct the climb. It provides an activity which balance strenght, technique, and knowledge to form a perfect puzzle, unlike any other sport. This combination is what makes such a unique and fun hobby of climbing.
